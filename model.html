Analyst Methodology — Why These Numbers
FCF Normalization
Rather than averaging past FCF, we use the FY2025 actual FCF of $22.97B as the base — the most current data from the FY2025 10-K. FY2022 ($36.1B) was excluded as a one-time Russia/Ukraine supply shock. Year 1 growth of +23.70% was derived analytically: the average of (FY2022 − FY2024)/FY2022 (11.02%) and (FY2022 − FY2025)/FY2022 (36.37%), proxying the Iran supply disruption recovery against the Russia shock template.
Growth Rate Justification
Year 1: +23.70% — derived from historical shock comparison (see above). Years 2–5: −10%/yr — consistent with XOM's observed post-Russia-shock FCF CAGR from FY2022 to FY2025 (approx. −14%/yr), moderated to −10% to reflect partial Iran recovery sustaining elevated prices longer than the Russia shock.
Terminal Growth Rate
2.5% — selected as the midpoint of WallStreetPrep's large-cap energy guidance (2.0%–3.5%), consistent with IMF long-run nominal US GDP growth forecasts. A higher rate was not used given structural energy-transition headwinds that may cap long-run growth for fossil fuel producers below GDP pace.
WACC Construction
Beta of 0.35 (Yahoo Finance, March 2026) reflects XOM's role as an inflation hedge — unusually low for an energy company, but validated by 5-year regression data. ERP of 4.46% uses Damodaran's January 2026 implied US ERP. Cost of debt at 1.39% pre-tax derived directly from FY2025 Interest Expense / Total Debt. Tax rate of 27.9% from FY2025 10-K (Income Tax Exp / EBT).
